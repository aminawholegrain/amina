 Storytelling

Imagine a city at night, with bright lights everywhere so you can't see the darkness. Those bright lights are your brain working, and everytime you hear a story at home and at school, there is a little city in your mind with lots of lights switching on! Your brain is imagining the story - whether you're going on a bearhunt, or playing with a talking snowman. Your brain is magical and powerful enough to imagine these stories, and that's a really nice thing. We would love it if you could draw us a story above, or maybe write us something too! You are the best storyteller already, keep going.

 Your Brain

You blink around 10,000 times a day. You laugh everytime a grown up says the word 'bogies', and that laugh literally lights up your brain. Since you were 1 day old, your brain has been working really hard with your heart to build patterns of emotions - this is why you cry when you can't get a new toy, or why you laugh when Buzz Lightyear wore a pink dress in Toy Story 2. Next time you feel bored, you should know that the little lights in your brain just need to be switched on again, and I know how! Jump up and down as fast as you can right now, for one minute. After a minute, sit down, close your eyes and place your hand on your heart. The lights are switching on now!
